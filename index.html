Before you board

Frequently Asked Questions

Dress code

Wear black.

One rule, and it's an easy one. How you wear it is up to you.

Black. Beyond that it's yours — silk, satin, leather, linen, tailored or relaxed. Two hard rules from the club, though: no shorts of any kind, and no slippers or slides.

Arrive in proper footwear — no slippers or slides at the gate. Once you're aboard, slippers are provided, so you can slip out of your heels for the night and still keep the decks clean. Wear something you can comfortably board and climb to the sky deck in.

Check-in opens at 21:00 at the Manila Yacht Club gate, and the first set starts right on the hour. Come 20–30 minutes early — the guestlist is checked name by name, and the yacht is docked, so there's no rush of missing a departure.

A valid government ID (18+, no exceptions) and your emailed boarding pass — printed or on your phone. Every guest under your booking must be named on the list, since the club requires the full manifest at the gate.

No — spots aren't transferable. Names are locked at booking and checked against the manifest at the gate, so only the guests you listed can board. Make sure every name is right before you confirm.

Yes — the VIP and SVIP couches take extra guests at ₱2,500 each, on top of the base pax. Add them at booking so their names make the manifest; walk-up additions can't be guaranteed once we hit 120.

Not inside the yacht — no cigarettes anywhere on the boat itself. There's a designated smoking area outside on the dock, so step off, light up there, and come back aboard.

All bookings are final — no refunds, no cancellations, no transfers. Spots are capped at 120 and yours is held the moment your payment clears, so double-check your package, your guest count, and every name before you confirm.
